Youth creator roles

Every project needs more than one kind of talent.

Young people can contribute based on their strengths. A project team might include a researcher, writer, artist, coder, tester, narrator, editor, or family-feedback lead.

RResearcher

Finds reliable facts and keeps source notes.

WWriter

Explains the lesson in clear, age-appropriate language.

AArtist

Creates illustrations, layouts, characters, or visual directions.

CCoder

Builds the interactive tool, game, or app logic.

TTester

Tries the project and reports what is confusing or broken.

EEditor

Checks clarity, spelling, privacy, sources, and final quality.

Safe creation rules

Create useful resources without exposing private family information.

1

Use adult support

An adult should review safety, privacy, source quality, and public sharing.

2

Use fictional examples

Do not publish private family stories, school records, health information, addresses, or account details.

3

Build safely

Use approved devices, accounts, materials, and age-appropriate tools. Read code before running it.

4

Cite sources

Use official or reliable sources and do not copy full copyrighted resources.

5

Test with real users

Ask family members what is clear, useful, respectful, and difficult.

6

Publish only after review

The current Creator Lab does not upload projects publicly or collect children’s submissions.
