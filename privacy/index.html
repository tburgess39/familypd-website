Privacy and Google permission transparency

FamilyPD Privacy Policy

Effective July 2026

Plain-language summary

  • Website worksheet entries are stored in the visitor’s browser.
  • Google Drive access is optional.
  • The app creates and manages only FamilyPD app-created files.
  • The app does not request Gmail access.
  • Users can revoke Google access and trash generated files.

1. Scope

This policy explains how Family Personal Development (“FamilyPD,” “we,” or “us”) handles information through the public website and the optional Google Drive workspace powered by Google Apps Script.

2. Information entered into website tools

FamilyPD tools may ask visitors to enter meeting notes, family priorities, mission and vision statements, roles, responsibilities, goals, or related information. By default, these entries are saved locally in the visitor’s browser using local storage. They are not automatically submitted to FamilyPD.

Clearing browser data, using a different browser or device, or selecting a tool’s clear/reset option may remove locally saved entries.

3. Optional Google Drive workspace

Visitors may choose to connect the FamilyPD website to a Google Drive workspace. This feature is optional and requires the visitor to sign in to Google and approve the permissions shown on Google’s consent screen.

When a visitor selects “Build My FamilyPD Workspace” or generates a PDF, the relevant tool information is sent to the authorized Google Apps Script web app so it can create folders, editable Google Docs, and PDF files in that visitor’s Drive.

4. Google permissions requested

The integration is designed to use limited permissions for files created or opened through the FamilyPD app and permission to create and edit Google Docs used for the visitor’s FamilyPD records.

The app does not request permission to read, send, or manage Gmail.

5. File control, retention, and removal

Generated files remain in the visitor’s Google Drive under that visitor’s control. The workspace manager may add folders and move FamilyPD app-created items to Google Drive Trash. It does not permanently erase files from Trash.

A visitor may also move, rename, archive, restore, or delete files directly through Google Drive, subject to Google’s own policies and account settings.

6. Revoking Google access

Visitors can revoke FamilyPD’s Google authorization through their Google Account security settings. Revoking access prevents future app actions but does not automatically delete files already created in Google Drive.

7. Technical and diagnostic information

Hosting providers and Google services may process standard technical information such as IP address, browser type, timestamps, authentication status, and error data. FamilyPD does not intentionally place private worksheet content into public logs.

8. Children and family information

Families should avoid entering highly sensitive information that is not necessary for the selected tool. Adults should use judgment before recording information about children, health, safety, finances, or other private household matters.

9. Third-party links

FamilyPD may link to news organizations, government agencies, community resources, Google services, and other third parties. Their privacy practices are governed by their own policies.

10. Security

FamilyPD uses reasonable technical and organizational safeguards, including limited Google Drive scopes, app-created file markers, per-user workspace state, and confirmation before destructive workspace actions. No system can guarantee absolute security.

11. Policy changes

This policy may be updated as FamilyPD services, legal requirements, or Google integrations change. The effective date will be updated when material revisions are made.

12. Contact

Questions about this policy may be sent to info@familypd.org.
