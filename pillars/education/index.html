Pillar Three

Education & Skills

Use learning and skills as a bridge to confidence, opportunity, and mobility.

Why this pillar matters

Education includes school, career and technical education, credentials, online learning, community programs, and the practical life skills taught at home.

What to pay attention to

  • What children are learning and where support is needed
  • School portals, deadlines, attendance, grades, IEPs, and 504 plans
  • Career interests, CTE, credentials, college, training, and apprenticeships
  • Adult learning, digital skills, reading, curiosity, and family teaching

Start small

  1. Choose one child or learning area to focus on.
  2. Schedule a ten-minute weekly education check-in.
  3. Pick one learning goal for an adult in the household.

Systems families can try

  • A shared school and learning calendar
  • Monthly progress and support review
  • A family learning block or teach-me-something night
  • A career and credential exploration list

Books mentioned in the Family PD guidebook

  • Linchpin — Seth Godin
  • Good to Great — Jim Collins
  • Dare to Lead — Brené Brown
Use books as conversation starters, not as substitutes for qualified professional support.
