Pillar Five

Goals

Give your family’s effort a clear direction without ignoring the foundation underneath it.

Why this pillar matters

Goals come last so they can be grounded in real health, relationships, skills, finances, capacity, and family values.

What to pay attention to

  • A small number of personal and shared family priorities
  • Specific results, realistic timeframes, and visible next steps
  • Support, obstacles, checkpoints, and adjustments
  • Celebrating effort and progress without tying worth to outcomes

Start small

  1. Choose one realistic personal goal for this season.
  2. Choose one shared family goal.
  3. Write both goals down and review them regularly.

Systems families can try

  • A monthly goal review
  • A visible goal board or tracker
  • A small-step action plan
  • A celebrate, learn, and adjust routine

Books mentioned in the Family PD guidebook

  • Think and Grow Rich — Napoleon Hill
  • The Alchemist — Paulo Coelho
  • Good to Great — Jim Collins
  • Greenlights — Matthew McConaughey
Use books as conversation starters, not as substitutes for qualified professional support.

Use all four guiding principles

Turn insight into a family system.

Self-awareness

What patterns, feelings, strengths, or barriers are affecting this pillar?

Systems

What repeatable routine would make the helpful action easier?

Policies

What expectation or boundary should the household agree on?

Community

Who or what trustworthy resource could provide support?

Important

Family PD provides general goal-setting education. Goals should be adjusted for health, safety, time, money, disability, and professional guidance.
