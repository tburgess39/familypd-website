Pillar Two

Relationships

Build relationships that support growth instead of slowly draining it.

Why this pillar matters

Relationships affect safety, opportunity, emotional health, family culture, and the support available when life gets difficult.

What to pay attention to

  • Respect, trust, honesty, and emotional safety
  • Communication, listening, conflict, and repair
  • Healthy boundaries and the ability to say no
  • Family, friends, mentors, school, work, and community networks

Start small

  1. Choose one relationship to strengthen with an intentional act.
  2. Set one small boundary with a relationship that repeatedly drains or harms you.
  3. Add gratitude, apology, or a feelings check-in to a family routine.

Systems families can try

  • A weekly relationship check-in
  • Clear expectations for respectful communication
  • A repair process after conflict
  • A family support and relationship-circle map

Books mentioned in the Family PD guidebook

  • Boundaries — Henry Cloud and John Townsend
  • The Let Them Theory — Mel Robbins
  • Dare to Lead — Brené Brown
  • The Laws of Human Nature — Robert Greene
Use books as conversation starters, not as substitutes for qualified professional support.
