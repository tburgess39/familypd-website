Pillar Four

Finances

Move from shame and survival toward awareness, stewardship, and stability.

Why this pillar matters

Finances are how money enters, leaves, protects, and supports the household—not simply how much money a family has.

What to pay attention to

  • Income, fixed and flexible expenses
  • Debt balances, interest, due dates, and credit reports
  • Emergency savings and short-term priorities
  • Insurance, benefits, documents, and backup plans
  • Feelings and habits connected to spending, earning, and asking for help

Start small

  1. Face one set of numbers: income, bills, balances, or debts.
  2. Change one manageable money habit this month.
  3. Set one short-term money goal and track it.

Systems families can try

  • A monthly family money meeting
  • A simple bill calendar and role assignment
  • A household purchase policy
  • One debt or savings tracker

Books mentioned in the Family PD guidebook

  • Think and Grow Rich — Napoleon Hill
  • Outwitting the Devil — Napoleon Hill
  • The Psychology of Money — Morgan Housel
Use books as conversation starters, not as substitutes for qualified professional support.
